## Task 2 - Influence of the atmospheric models (El > 5°)

<img width="1338" height="1153" alt="task2_enu_atmos" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ef3d1d7f-3b5e-46f7-aad0-55b2097dba4c" />


<img width="751" height="795" alt="task2_scatter" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6e1c7c73-a054-4e05-8826-946bb8938d89" />



| case | E mean | E std | N mean | N std | **U mean** | U std |
|------|-------:|------:|-------:|------:|-----------:|------:|
| a) no model | −0.483 | 0.583 | 1.897 | 0.684 | **+23.23** | 1.467 |
| b) tropo only (Saastamoinen) | −0.363 | 0.130 | 1.407 | 0.269 | **+12.63** | 0.659 |
| c) iono only (Klobuchar) | −0.130 | 0.531 | 0.130 | 0.511 | **+10.43** | 1.701 |
| d) both models | 0.158 | 0.174 | −0.206 | 0.276 | **≈0.00** | 0.430 |

(all values in metres; U mean = vertical offset relative to the both-models solution)

**Interpretation.** Atmospheric delays make the measured pseudoranges *too long*.
Uncorrected, this pushes the estimated position **upward**:

- **No model (a):** worst case, **+23 m** vertical bias - the sum of the neglected
  ionospheric (~13 m) and tropospheric (~11 m) delays.
- **Tropo only (b):** removes the tropospheric part → bias drops to ~+12.6 m, and the
  vertical noise (U std) is halved.
- **Iono only (c):** removes the ionospheric part → bias ~+10.4 m.
- **Both (d):** bias eliminated (≈0 by definition of the reference) and the smallest
  spread (U std 0.43 m, horizontal RMS 0.42 m) → the correct, fully-corrected solution.

The atmosphere affects mainly the **vertical (Up)** coordinate; the horizontal E/N
means shift by only a few decimetres. **Conclusion:** enabling both models is essential
for accurate height estimation.

### Which factors are affected by the elevation mask?

Raising the mask **removes low-elevation satellites**, which propagates through the
whole solution. The factors that change are:

1. **Number of usable satellites** - drops from 8.77 (5°) to 7.04 (15°). Fewer
   observations → less redundancy.
2. **Satellite geometry / DOP** - with the constellation cut down, PDOP rises
   0.94 → 1.38, VDOP 0.82 → 1.23, HDOP 0.46 → 0.61. The **vertical** geometry
   degrades most, because low-elevation satellites are precisely the ones that
   constrain height.
3. **Position scatter (std) and coordinate means** - worse geometry increases the
   vertical noise (U std 0.43 → 0.53 m) and shifts the height (U mean +0.44 m at 15°).
   The horizontal means move only at the few-cm/dm level.
4. **Solution availability** - a high mask can leave < 4 satellites at some epochs,
   producing *no* fix. This is the extreme illustrated by El > 30°.
